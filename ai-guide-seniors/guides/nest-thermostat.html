Getting Started

1 Download the Google Home App

On your smartphone or tablet, open the App Store (iPhone/iPad) or Google Play Store (Android).

Search for "Google Home" and download it (free!).

2 Sign In with Google

Open the Google Home app and sign in with your Google account.

Don't have one? You can create a free Google account in the app.

3 Add Your Nest Thermostat

If your Nest is already installed, tap the "+" icon in the Google Home app.

Tap "Set up device" ‚Üí "Works with Google" ‚Üí search for "Nest".

Follow the prompts to link your Nest account (or create one).

Need installation help? HVAC technicians or handymen can install Nest thermostats professionally.

4 Test It Out

In the Google Home app, tap your thermostat.

Try raising or lowering the temperature‚Äîyou should see the change on the physical thermostat!

Simple Things to Try

Change Temperature from Your Phone

  1. Open the Google Home app
  2. Find your thermostat (it'll show the current temperature)
  3. Tap it to open controls
  4. Drag the temperature slider up or down
  5. That's it! The thermostat updates immediately

Set a Schedule

Save energy by setting when to heat or cool your home:

  1. Tap the thermostat in the Google Home app
  2. Tap the gear icon (settings)
  3. Tap "Schedule"
  4. Set temperatures for different times (e.g., cooler at night, warmer in morning)

Use "Eco Mode" When Away

Going on vacation or out for the day?

  1. Open the Google Home app ‚Üí tap your thermostat
  2. Tap "Eco"
  3. Nest will use less energy until you return
  4. Turn it off when you're home again

Common Questions

What if the app says "Offline"?

Your Nest lost Wi-Fi connection. Check that your internet is working, and try restarting your router. The thermostat will still work manually until Wi-Fi reconnects.

Can I control it from my computer?

Yes! Go to home.google.com and sign in. You'll see all your Google Home devices including your Nest.

What's the difference between Heat, Cool, and Heat‚Ä¢Cool?

  • Heat: Only heating (winter mode)
  • Cool: Only cooling (summer mode)
  • Heat‚Ä¢Cool: Automatically heats or cools to stay in a comfortable range

How do I save the most energy?

Use Eco mode when away, set a schedule that lowers temp at night, and let Nest learn your patterns. The app shows your energy usage and tips!
